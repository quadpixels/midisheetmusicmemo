MidiSheetMusic Memo

Online documentation: http://edgeofmap.com/blog/midisheetmusic-memo-en/

A MIDI sheet music memorization app and a MIDI player, based on MidiSheetMusic from Madhav Vaidyanathan.

This app is based on the idea of memorization from the book Fundamentals of Piano Practice by Chuan C. Chang.

[About in-app help screens]

There are in-app help screens. They show up once then disappear.

To bring them up again after they disappear, click the button on the top of this window.

[About mastery]

Tiles (a measure in a staff) are grouped into different piles. At the beginning of learning all tiles go to the first pile, meaning they are totally fresh to you. As you gradually progress, tiles will be moved to another pile depending on whether you have made it correct (along a green arrow) or wrong (along a red arrow) for the first attempt after it appears in quiz mode. A tile that has been moved to a green pile is considered completely mastered. Its mastery level will not change after being moved to a green pile.

 

The numbers in each of the tiles represent the number of tiles in each pile.

A tile is moved along a green or red arrow depending on whether you have made a correct answer in the first attempt in the quiz mode. A tile that has been moved into a green circle is considered completely mastered.

The segments in the histogram represents the number of tiles in each pile as shown in the illustration. The colors correspond to the colors of the piles.

There are green piles which are identical but are reached along different paths. The consideration is:

  • •If you are very familiar with a piece of sheet music, it must be easy for you to make correct first attempts for three times in a row, moving a tile into the green pile on the top, or 

  • •If you are not so familiar with a piece of sheet music such that you made a mistake which prevents you from reaching the first green pile, you need to take more quizees to reinforce the correct memory, and only claim full mastery of a tile after you have made three correct first attempts in quiz mode. 

Therefore, it takes at least three correct first attempts to reach the first green pile and 5 correct first attempts to reach the second green pile.

Mastery of most of the measures is a good indication that you are likely to perform this piece with a musical instrument without flubs or blackouts.

While the mastery graph gives a rough idea of your mastery of this piece of music, you may also want to identify weaknesses with a more detailed overlay view as described in the next section.

[About perspective (combining multiple staffs to two staffs)]

This is a feature in MidiSheetMusic (MSM). As this app is a direct spin-off of MSM, this feature is preserved in verbatim.

When a sheet music contains more than two staffs you may let MSM combine it into two staffs.

This results in a different perspective into the same sheet music, which looks a visually different one, although it contains the same notes as the original does.

Since the notes inside each tile would change due to the combination, their mastery levels are treated differently.

[About overlays]

To switch between overlay modes, click the third button on the bottom tool bar. An overlay is a vertical bar on top of the music score to show statistics of each measure of each staff obtained from the quiz mode, which is a numerical value. The taller the bar is, the higher the value it represents is. For example:

 

Example: the aggregate accuracy of the Measure 6 of the first staff is 50%.

Statistics are colored green, red or blue. (varies with the color scheme chosen)

A higher green statistic means a better mastery of this sheet music. The green statistics are:

  • •Accuracy 

  • •Mastery Level 

On the contrary, a lower red statistic means a better mastery of this sheet music. The red statistics are:

  • •Overall response time 

  • •Accuracy-corrected effective overall response time 

Blue statistics are neutral and are not relevant to mastery of this sheet music. There is only one of this kind:

  • •Occurrences 

All of the occurrences mentioned above are normalized to [0, 1].

[About statistics]

The meaning of the statistics (time and accuracy) are self-evident.

Statistics of different perspectives are combined and aggregated together. They are not separated because a sheet music with N staffs can have 2^N different perspective, which may be too many.

[Color scheme explanation]

Choice of color scheme is reflected in the file list, similar to a “bookmark”. For example, you can mark the “sheet music being learned recently” as a specific color.

[Why do I make this app?]

I'm beginning to teach myself piano and I came across the book Fundamentals of Piano Practice. During the progress I discover barriers towards the fulfillment of being able to perform a piece and record it with a video camera, some of which include playing form the middle of a piece, and practice bar-by-bar.

The memorization step does not require a piano teacher and does not even necessarily need physical access to a piano and can be helped by a program/machine.

This app is an attempt to make the process of memorization more traceable and enjoyable.

[Why is memorizing sheet music important?]

A person who is able to perform in front of people or a video camera would have memorized the sheet music no matter he chooses to do so or not because the technical requirements makes it necessary to memorize. According to the book, "For practically all students, the most difficult passages are played from memory". (Page 104)

Therefore, memorization is one important step in the music work flow and enables many other steps. To look beyond, these are some other steps mentioned in this book that you would (in)consciously follow:

Graphics/Music/Hand Memorization → Play from middle of a sheet music → Mental Play → Confidence and Fault-Free Performance

The quiz mode of this app tries to help the first two with graphics memorization.

[Who is this app for?]

I wanted to share with all those who are struggling and also deriving satisfaction from teaching themselves piano. The technological limits of this app is the limit of the original MidiSheetMusic, which is capable of parsing and presenting sheet music of the complexity of Bach's Two-Part Inventions. The Inventions are also used as examples in the book Fundamentals of Piano Practice, so I expect this app to go a long way with a piano beginner like me.
